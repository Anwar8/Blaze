◆ calc_axes()

void ElementConfiguration::calc_axes ( )
inline

calculates the unit vectors x_axis, y_axis, and z_axis.

based on sensible-looking: https://stackoverflow.com/questions/14607640/rotating-a-vector-in-3d-space Rotation around z (to get y-axis from x-axis):

\( \boldsymbol{X}^{zz=90 ^{\circ}} = \boldsymbol{T}\boldsymbol{X} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & -1 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 & 0\\ 0 & 0 & 1\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -y \\ x \\ z \end{bmatrix}\)

Rotation around y (to get z-axis from x-axis):

\( \boldsymbol{X}^{yy=90 ^{\circ}} = \boldsymbol{T}\boldsymbol{X} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 0\\ -1 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \\ z \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} z \\ y \\ -x \end{bmatrix}\)

Warning
This rotation assumes "Y" is the vertical (gravity) axis. Local axis assumes local z-axis is into the page.
